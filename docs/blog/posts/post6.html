QR Code Scannability: Contrast, Quiet Zone, and Module Size Requirements

Published: November 20, 2025

Even with perfect data and H-level error correction, a QR code can fail to scan. Here’s why — and how to avoid it.

ISO/IEC 18004 Minimum Requirements

  • Quiet Zone: 4 modules on all sides (not 3, not 2)
  • Contrast: ≥70% between dark and light modules
  • Minimum Module Size: 0.25 mm (0.01 in) for standard scanners

Real-World Failures

  • Printed on glossy card: reflection kills contrast
  • White border 4 modules: scanner can’t find timing patterns
  • Inverted colors (light on dark): most apps fail
  • Printed at 0.3 mm/module: phone cameras struggle

Recommended Print Settings

  • Size: Minimum 25×25 mm (1×1 in) for business cards
  • Quiet zone: 5–6 modules for extra safety
  • Colors: Pure black (#000000) on pure white (#FFFFFF)
  • Paper: Matte or uncoated

Tested Scanner Tolerance

iOS Camera: fails at 0.22 mm/module
Google Lens: fails at 0.18 mm/module
Dedicated scanners: work down to 0.15 mm/module

Conclusion

Error correction fixes data damage — not optical failure. Follow the physical standards, and your QR code will scan every time.

The best QR code is the one that scans on the first try.
