Error Correction Levels: L, M, Q, or H

Error Correction Levels: When to Use L, M, Q, or H in Practice

Published: November 20, 2025

QR codes support four error correction levels defined in ISO/IEC 18004. Here’s exactly when to use each — based on real scanner testing.

Error Correction Capacity

  • L (Low): ~7% damage recovery
  • M (Medium): ~15% (default in many tools)
  • Q (Quartile): ~25%
  • H (High): ~30% — recommended for professional use

Real-World Damage Tolerance

Tested with iOS Camera, Google Lens, and dedicated scanners:

  • H-level QR survives 28% obscuration (e.g. logo overlay, dirt)
  • Q-level fails at ~23%
  • M-level fails at ~14%
  • L-level fails at ~6%

Version & Data Impact

A 500-byte vCard:

  • L: Version 6 (29×29 modules)
  • M: Version 7 (33×33)
  • Q: Version 8 (37×37)
  • H: Version 9 (41×41) ← still very scannable

When to Choose Each Level

  • H: Business cards, posters, outdoor signs — best reliability
  • Q: Indoor signage, high-density prints
  • M: Digital-only (email signatures, websites)
  • L: Only if payload >2KB and scannability is secondary

Recommendation

Use H error correction for all professional contact QR codes. The slight increase in size is negligible compared to the massive gain in real-world reliability.

A QR code that can’t be scanned is worthless — prioritize robustness.
